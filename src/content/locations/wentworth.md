Wentworth is a historic town at the junction of the Murray and Darling rivers, and its housing reflects that age. Federation-era cottages, river-side weatherboards, post-war additions and a layer of modern infill all coexist within a small footprint. It's also a town where flood considerations sit close to the surface — literally and figuratively.

Heritage stock needs heritage-aware inspection. A 1920s cottage will fail every modern building code in dozens of small ways — most of which are not actually problems (the home is permitted to remain as built), but some of which need to be on your radar before you buy. Original wiring, original plumbing, original brickwork on flexible footings, lead paint, asbestos cement on later additions — all of these are normal findings on a Wentworth heritage home, and what matters is whether they're stable and safe.

The Wayfare report on a Wentworth property is generally longer than average. Not because the home is worse, but because there's more story to tell. We photograph generously, rate honestly, and explain what each finding means for a buyer.
