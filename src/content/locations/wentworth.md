Wentworth is a historic river town at the junction of the Murray and Darling rivers, about thirty-five minutes from Mildura. It was one of the earliest inland river ports in Australia, central to the paddle-steamer trade through the 1800s, and that history is still visible in the town's layout, public buildings and housing.

The housing reflects more than a century of growth. Federation-era cottages, riverside weatherboards, post-war additions and a layer of modern infill coexist within a small footprint. Block sizes are generally generous, and many properties sit on land that has been continuously occupied for over a hundred years. Flood considerations are part of life in Wentworth — literally and figuratively — and worth being aware of before you buy.
