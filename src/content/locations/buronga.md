Buronga is just across the bridge from Mildura, and it shares Gol Gol's profile in many ways: newer estate housing, NSW jurisdiction, fast-growing community, and a price point that has historically tempted Mildura buyers across the river.

Like any rapid-growth estate area, the defects worth catching in Buronga tend to be in the things that get rushed under deadline pressure — flashings, garage detailing, slab edge protection, drainage falls, render finish. Cosmetically these homes look great. The Wayfare inspection looks past the cosmetic, into the spots where time and rushed workmanship show up first.

For older Buronga stock, particularly the cottages on lower-lying blocks closer to the river, sub-floor moisture is the perennial item. Stumped homes need ventilation that actually moves air — many don't, and the consequences sit hidden under the floor.
