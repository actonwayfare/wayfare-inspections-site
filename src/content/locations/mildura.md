Mildura is the largest town in the Sunraysia region, founded in the late 1880s as Australia's first major irrigation settlement under the Chaffey brothers. The town grew on the back of fruit, vines and the rail link to Melbourne, and that progression shows in successive layers of housing as you move from the original riverside grid out toward the newer estates on the edges.

Almost every era of Australian residential building is represented here. Pre-war weatherboard cottages near the river, brick-veneer subdivisions from the 1960s and 70s, fibro-and-tile homes in the older suburbs, project homes from the 1990s and 2000s, and a steady belt of new-build estates further out. Block sizes are generous in the established parts of town; modern subdivisions on the fringes tend to run tighter.
