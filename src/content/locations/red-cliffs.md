Red Cliffs was established as a soldier-settlement community after the First World War, with returned servicemen allocated small irrigation blocks to work. A lot of the original housing dates from that period or shortly after, and the town still carries the layout of a planned settlement — wide streets, small original cottages on regular blocks, a tight town centre surrounded by orchard country.

The housing stock is a mix of those original fibro-and-weatherboard cottages, many of them extended and modified over the generations, alongside original timber-framed homes and a layer of newer builds on the edges of town. Block sizes vary from the small original soldier-settlement allocations to much larger rural and lifestyle blocks further out.
